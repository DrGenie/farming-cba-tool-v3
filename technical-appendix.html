What the tool does

You upload your trial data. The tool averages replicates within each treatment, compares each treatment to the control, and reports the economic difference using a consistent set of assumptions.

The workflow

  1. Download the template and enter your data.
  2. Mark at least one row as the control.
  3. Upload, then run analysis.
  4. Read the leaderboard and plots.
  5. Export a short report if needed.

Minimum data needed

  • At least one control row and at least one treatment row.
  • A treatment name (same name for all replicates of that treatment).
  • Yield in tonnes per hectare.
  • Total cost per hectare.

Worked example

Suppose a treatment has 4 replicate plots with yields of 7.3, 6.3, 7.6 and 7.7 t/ha. The tool averages them to 7.23 t/ha.

If grain price is $500/t, annual benefit is 7.23 × 500 = $3,615 per hectare. If the annual cost is $885/ha, the net per year is $2,730/ha.

The tool then discounts future years and sums them to a present value so all treatments are comparable.

What “discounting” means in practice

Discounting just means that benefits and costs in later years count a bit less than the same dollar today. The tool applies the same rate to all treatments, so rankings are consistent.

Understanding the tabs

  • Home: what the tool does and the steps.
  • Data: upload your file, check what was detected, set the control and assumptions.
  • Results: leaderboard and tables. Run analysis here.
  • Plots: charts for quick comparison.
  • Export: download report and cleaned data.
If something does not look right, check the Data tab first. Most issues are caused by a missing control mark, inconsistent treatment names, or numbers entered with commas or symbols.
